Liège style meatballs

Description

A traditional dish from my hometown, usually served with fries and a salad. Its preparation requires the use of real Liège syrup, a typical ingredient of the region.

Ingredients

4 servings

For the meatballs :

  • 500g minced beef
  • 500g minced pork
  • 4 slices of white bread (without the crust) soaked in milk
  • 1 finely chopped onion
  • 1 bunch of finely chopped parsley
  • 2 eggs
  • salt
  • pepper
  • nutmeg
  • breadcrumbs

For the sauce :

  • 4 onions
  • thyme
  • 4 tablespoons of brown sugar
  • red wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons of real Liège syrup
  • 4 cloves
  • juniper berries
  • salt
  • pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • cornstarch or kneaded butter (50% butter mixed with 50% flour)

Steps

Preparing meatballs :

  1. Knead the above ingredients until smooth
  2. Roll the meballs to about 120 g each
  3. Bake in a greased baking tray for approximately 40 minutes

Preparing the sauce :

  1. In the cooking juice, brown 4 chopped onions, sprinkle with a pinch of thyme, let brown.
  2. Add 4 tablespoons of brown sugar to form a caramel, then deglaze with a dash of red wine vinegar.
  3. Moisten with 1 liter of meat broth.
  4. Bring to a boil and add 2 good tablespoons of real Liège syrup and 4 cloves as well as a few juniper berries.
  5. Season with salt and pepper and 2 bay leaves. Cook for 30 to 35 minutes.
  6. At the end of cooking, add a few currants and thicken the sauce with cornstarch or with kneaded butter (50% butter mixed with 50% flour).
  7. Adjust the seasoning.
  8. Place the meatballs in the sauce and simmer for a few minutes over very low heat.
